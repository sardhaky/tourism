Gateway of India

Mumbai, India

The Gateway of India is an arch-monument built in the early 20th century in Mumbai, India. It was erected to commemorate the landing of King George V and Queen Mary at Apollo Bunder during their visit to India in 1911.

Key Facts

  • Construction was completed in 1924.
  • Architectural style: Indo-Saracenic, with influences from Gujarati architecture.
  • A prominent tourist attraction and historical landmark of Mumbai.
