Qutub Minar

Delhi, India

The Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the tallest brick minaret in the world. It is located in Delhi, India, and is one of the finest examples of Indo-Islamic architecture.

Key Facts

  • Construction started in 1192 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak and was completed by his successor, Iltutmish.
  • The minaret is 72.5 meters high with 379 steps to the top.
  • The complex includes several other historical structures, such as the Iron Pillar.
