Hawa Mahal

Jaipur, India

Hawa Mahal, also known as the "Palace of Winds," is a unique five-story palace in Jaipur, India, known for its distinctive architectural style with rows of small windows designed to allow royal ladies to observe street festivals without being seen.

Key Facts

  • Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh.
  • Made from red and pink sandstone, typical of Rajasthani architecture.
  • The palace has 953 small windows, called jharokhas, which allow air to flow through, keeping the palace cool.
  • A popular tourist attraction and a prominent symbol of Jaipur's rich cultural heritage.
